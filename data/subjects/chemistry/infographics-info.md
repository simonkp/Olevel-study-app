#### Topic 1: Experimental design
### File: matter-01-experimental-design.jpg

**Fair test = one change, one measure, everything else same.**  
This poster shows how to plan an experiment fairly. You **change only one thing** — the **independent variable** (e.g., acid concentration). You then **measure the result** — the **dependent variable** (e.g., rate of reaction or time taken), shown by the timer and gauge. The arrows mean the change you make can affect what you measure.

To keep it fair, **all other factors stay constant** — the **controlled variables** like **temperature** (thermometer), **volume** of solutions, and **catalyst** amount. In short: **change 1 → measure 1 → keep the rest the same.** This prevents other factors from messing up your results so your conclusion is reliable.

#### Topic 2: Purification
### File: matter-02-purification.jpg

**Simple distillation (left):** Heat a liquid mixture so the part with the **lowest boiling point** turns to gas first. The gas goes through the **condenser** (cold water in, warm water out), cools back to liquid, and is collected as **pure distillate**. The **thermometer** shows the boiling point; **boiling chips** help smooth boiling. Use this to **purify a liquid** (e.g., water from salty water) or separate liquids with very different boiling points.

**Paper chromatography (right):** Put small **ink/solute spots** on the **baseline** of the paper. The **solvent** climbs up; different substances move **different distances**, forming separate **colored spots** below the **solvent front**. The **lid** reduces evaporation. Use this to **separate and identify** components in a mixture (e.g., dyes in ink).  
**Shortcut:** Distillation = *purify a liquid*. Chromatography = *separate/identify parts of a mixture*.

#### Topic 3: Kinetic theory
### File: matter-03-kinetic-theory.jpg

**Solid (ordered grid of spheres):** The particles are close together and tightly packed, like a grid. They vibrate around fixed positions but don't move much.
**Liquid (disordered close):** The particles are close together but can slide past each other. They move around a lot but still stay near each other.
**Gas (sparse fast arrows):** The particles are far apart and move very fast. They can move in any direction and don't stay near each other.
